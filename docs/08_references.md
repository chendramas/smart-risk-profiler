## Framework & Metodologi Utama

### CFA Institute IRP Framework

> Hubble, T., Grable, J., & Dannhauser, Z. (2020). *Investment Risk Profiling: A Guide for Financial Advisors*. CFA Institute Research Foundation. Charlottesville, VA: CFA Institute.

- **Status:** Standar emas (gold standard) untuk risk profiling profesional
- **Kontribusi:** 3-Factor IRP Model (Risk Need, Risk-Taking Ability, Behavioral Loss Tolerance)
- **Prinsip kunci:** "The binding constraint governs"
- **Sistem:** 27-combination traffic light system

### Risk Tolerance Scale

> Grable, J. & Lytton, R. (1999). "Financial Risk Tolerance Revisited: The Development of a Risk Assessment Instrument." *Financial Services Review*, 8(3): 163-181.

- **Status:** Klasik, widely cited (1,200+ citations)
- **Kontribusi:** Skala pengukuran risk tolerance yang menjadi dasar banyak risk profiler modern
- **Metode:** 13-item questionnaire dengan validasi psychometric

## Behavioral Finance & Prospect Theory

### Prospect Theory

> Kahneman, D. & Tversky, A. (1979). "Prospect Theory: An Analysis of Decision under Risk." *Econometrica*, 47(2): 263-291.

- **Status:** Landasan behavioral economics (50,000+ citations)
- **Kontribusi:** Loss aversion coefficient (λ ≈ 2.0-2.5), S-shaped value function
- **Prinsip kunci:** "Losses loom larger than gains"

### Cumulative Prospect Theory

> Tversky, A. & Kahneman, D. (1992). "Advances in Prospect Theory: Cumulative Representation of Uncertainty." *Journal of Risk and Uncertainty*, 5(4): 297-323.

- **Status:** Pengembangan Prospect Theory (15,000+ citations)
- **Kontribusi:** Cumulative weighting function, refinement of loss aversion measurement

### Aspects of Investor Behavior

> Kahneman, D. & Riepe, M. (1998). "Aspects of Investor Behavior." *Journal of Portfolio Management*, 24(4): 52-65.

- **Status:** Highly cited dalam behavioral finance
- **Kontribusi:** Overconfidence causes overestimation of knowledge, illusion of control
- **Kutipan kunci:** *"The illusion of knowledge — the feeling that one understands the world — is reinforced by the amount of information one has."*

## Loss Aversion

### Loss Aversion Properties

> Walasek, L. & Stewart, T. (2014). "How to Make Loss Aversion Disappear and Reverse: Tests of the Decision by Sampling Origin of Loss Aversion." *Journal of Experimental Psychology: General*, 143(1): 391-402.

- **Kontribusi:** Menunjukkan bahwa loss aversion adalah property of experimental design, bukan trait tetap
- **Implikasi:** Perlu multiple scenarios untuk mengukur loss aversion secara robust

### Loss Aversion Questionnaire

> De Baets, S. (2012). *Loss Aversion Questionnaire (LAQ)*. Unpublished instrument.

- **Kontribusi:** Kuesioner spesifik untuk mengukur loss aversion dalam domain finansial
- **Metode:** Likert-scale items yang mengukur sensitivity terhadap loss vs gain

## Financial Literacy & Overconfidence

### Financial Literacy Research

> Baker, H. Kent et al. (multiple publications, 609 citations cumulative). Financial literacy and investment behavior research.

- **Kontribusi:** Financial literacy predicts overconfidence; paradox — medium literacy = highest overconfidence
- **Relevansi:** Menjelaskan mengapa consistency checks diperlukan

## Risk Profiling di Indonesia

### Robo-Advisor Risk Profiling

> Hasanah, E.N., Wiryono, S.K., & Koesrindartoto, D.P. (2024). "Risk profiling question investigation for robo-advisor." *Business, Management and Economics Engineering*, 22(2): 382-400. DOI: 10.3846/bmee.2024.21182

- **Afiliasi:** Institut Teknologi Bandung (ITB)
- **Kontribusi:** Menemukan bahwa emergency fund status, home ownership, dan source of funds adalah pertanyaan paling kritis untuk risk profiling di Indonesia
- **Metode:** Investigation of risk profiling questions used by robo-advisors

### Cultural Influences on Risk Tolerance

> ResearchGate (Januari 2026). "Cultural influences on risk tolerance and portfolio creation: Evidence from Indonesian students."

- **Kontribusi:** Mahasiswa Indonesia menunjukkan risk tolerance lebih rendah dari mahasiswa AS; faktor budaya signifikan
- **Metode:** Cross-cultural comparison

### Cultural Norms & Risk Tolerance

> Asia Pacific Journal (2026). Cultural norms and risk tolerance in Southeast Asia.

- **Kontribusi:** Norma budaya di Asia Tenggara secara signifikan membentuk risk tolerance
- **Temuan:** Social proof lebih kuat di budaya collectivist

## Net Worth & Portfolio Allocation

> Grable, J. & Chatterjee, S. (2026). [Financial Planning Research]. *Journal of Financial Planning*, 39(1): 60-79.

- **Kontribusi:** Net worth adalah prediktor terkuat dari alokasi portofolio (p < .001)
- **Signifikansi:** Lebih kuat dari age, income, atau education

## Overconfidence & Trading Behavior

### Trading is Hazardous to Your Wealth

> Barber, B. & Odean, T. (2000). "Trading Is Hazardous to Your Wealth: The Common Stock Investment Performance of Individual Investors." *Journal of Finance*, 55(2): 773-806.

- **Status:** Klasik (8,000+ citations)
- **Kontribusi:** Overconfident investors trade too frequently and underperform

### Boys Will Be Boys

> Barber, B. & Odean, T. (2001). "Boys Will Be Boys: Gender, Overconfidence, and Common Stock Investment." *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, 116(1): 261-292.

- **Kontribusi:** Gender differences in overconfidence and trading behavior

## Industry Risk Profiling Products

### Wealthfront

> Wealthfront (2020). *Classic Portfolio Investment Methodology White Paper*. Palo Alto, CA: Wealthfront Corporation.

- **Kontribusi:** Overconfidence inflates risk tolerance scores by 15-25%; consistency check methodology
- **Metode:** Risk parity + behavioral adjustment
- **Akses:** [wealthfront.com/whitepaper](https://wealthfront.com/whitepaper)

### Oxford Risk / FinaMetrica

> FinaMetrica (now part of Oxford Risk). *Risk Profiling System*. 1.7 million tests administered across 35+ countries.

- **Kontribusi:** Largest empirical dataset on risk tolerance globally
- **Metode:** 25-item psychometric questionnaire
- **Skor:** Risk tolerance score 0-100

### Nitrogen / Riskalyze

> Nitrogen Wealth (formerly Riskalyze). *Risk Number® Methodology*.

- **Kontribusi:** Risk Number® 1-99 scale; gamified risk assessment
- **Metode:** Based on academic research (Kahneman & Tversky), simplified for advisors
- **Market:** US financial advisors

### StashAway ERAA®

> StashAway. *ERAA® (Economic Regime-based Asset Allocation) Methodology*.

- **Kontribusi:** AI-based risk profiling for Southeast Asian investors
- **Metode:** Economic regime analysis + behavioral assessment
- **Market:** Singapore, Malaysia, Thailand, Hong Kong

## Dynamic Risk Tolerance

> Thornton, J. (multiple publications, 763 citations cumulative). Dynamic risk tolerance research.

- **Kontribusi:** Risk tolerance is not static — it changes with market conditions, life events, and age
- **Implikasi:** Risk profiling should be repeated periodically, not done once

## Regulatory & Industry Studies

### Ontario Securities Commission (2015)

> Ontario Securities Commission (2015). *Canadian Securities Administrators — Suitability and Know Your Client Research*.

- **Temuan:** Only 16.7% of risk tolerance questionnaires were "fit for purpose"
- **Kontribusi:** Menunjukkan kelemahan mayoritas risk profiler yang ada

### EY (2020)

> Ernst & Young (2020). "Is your risk profiling process detecting biases, or enabling them?" *EY Financial Services*.

- **Kontribusi:** Analisis bagaimana risk profiler bisa **meng-enable** bias alih-alih mendeteksinya
- **Relevansi:** Mendukung penggunaan consistency checks dan behavioral scenarios
